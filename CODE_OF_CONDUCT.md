# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

SnoutDB contributors, maintainers, and users are expected to make
participation respectful, professional, and constructive.

Positive behavior includes:

- discussing technical decisions with evidence and clear reasoning;
- giving actionable feedback about code rather than people;
- welcoming questions and different levels of experience;
- acknowledging mistakes and correcting them;
- respecting privacy, boundaries, and differing viewpoints;
- keeping project spaces focused on improving SnoutDB.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language or unwanted attention;
- deliberate intimidation, stalking, or disclosure of private information;
- trolling, repeated disruption, or bad-faith argument;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern;
- conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, code
review, project chat, events, and public interactions where someone represents
SnoutDB or its community.

## Reporting

Do not report sensitive conduct issues in a public issue. Contact the
repository owner privately using the contact information available on their
GitHub profile. Include relevant links, dates, context, and any supporting
material.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. Anyone
handling a report must disclose conflicts of interest and step aside when
necessary.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take actions including:

1. A private clarification or warning.
2. Removal or editing of inappropriate content.
3. Temporary restriction from project participation.
4. Permanent removal from project spaces.

The response will consider severity, impact, context, prior behavior, and
whether the person takes responsibility and corrects the conduct.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this policy in good faith may be
removed from their project role.
